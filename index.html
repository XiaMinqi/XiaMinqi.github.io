Research  Interests

  • Constant Potential Method in electrocatalysis 
    The development of high-performance electrocatalysts for water splitting is an ideal solution to the energy and environment issue. In the past few years, computational electrochemical method has become an essential approach to obtain atomic- resolution mechanism of catalysts. However, current theoretical descriptions of interface chemical processes are mostly based on computational hydrogen electrode (CHE) model, where electron transfer caused by potential diversification and the resulting electric field effects and d-band shift cannot be described precisely. Therefore, application of constant potential method (CPM) is of significance to promote understanding of charge effects in electrocatalytic materials. 
  • Molecular dynamics of nanofluids
    The field of nanofluidics explores the transport of fluids and ionic species at the nanometric scales. In an interesting contrast to solid-state physics — where nanoscale behavior has long been studied — the exploration of hydrodynamics and related transport at the smallest scales has only emerged over the past 15 years. To explore the direction for the preparation and regulation of carbon nanocages, we are going to use molecular dynamics (MD) methods to study the process of pore formation and the transport mechanism of substances within the pore respectively.
